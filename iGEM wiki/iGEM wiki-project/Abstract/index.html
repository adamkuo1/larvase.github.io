Abstract

Many people believe that studying abroad provides valuable educational and cultural experiences. Students who travel to other countries for their education are exposed to new teaching methods, languages, and customs. This not only improves their academic skills but also helps them develop independence and adaptability. For example, a student from Taiwan studying in the United States may learn to manage daily life in a different language while adjusting to unfamiliar classroom expectations. These challenges ultimately prepare students for global careers and cross-cultural communication.
